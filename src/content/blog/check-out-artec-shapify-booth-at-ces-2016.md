08 Apr 2015


Las Vegas is not only famous for its casinos – in early January the city hosts one of the largest and longest-running trade shows for innovations and consumer technologies, the [International CES®](http://www.cesweb.org/) (Consumer Electronics Show). According to the organizers, the 2015 CES was the largest show in history with over 2.2 million net square feet of exhibit space featuring more than 3,600 exhibitors, including a record number of startups.

![](http://blog.shapify.me/content/images/2015/Dec/CES.jpg)

Artec will be bringing its instant full-body 3D scanning system, Shapify Booth, to CES 2016 to make 3D portraits of attendees, also called shapies. Shapify showcases Artec’s ultimate automatic full-body scanning technology taking only 12 seconds to scan a person and 15 minutes to produce a ready-for-print 3D model in brilliant color. A Shapify Booth can become a perfect revenue booster for parks, shopping malls, airports and other venues with a heavy footfall as people are beginning to embrace the new mini-me trend.

If you want to get a 3D printed mini-you, come to our stand to get scanned in the Shapify Booth, then just choose the figurine size you want and leave us your shipping address – you’ll receive your figurine a few weeks later. Figurine prices start at $169 for an S-size figurine (6"/15cm). An M-size figurine (7.5"/19cm) will cost you $219 and an L (9"/23cm) is worth $299. Delivery is $35.

3D portraits can also be shared on Facebook – every person scanned in the Shapify Booth will receive a free preview of their shapie by email so they can embed it in a Facebook post. Ready for print shapies can be downloaded from the Shapify website for $40.

Where: Stand #72523, Halls A-C, Sands, Tech West, Las Vegas, Nevada, United States   
When: January 6-9
